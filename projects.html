PDAC TME Modeling

Multi-agent modeling of the pancreatic tumor microenvironment and spatial network analysis of patient tissue data.

GitHub
PhysiCellC++PythonSpatial OmicsmIF / IMCMarkov Random Fields
Context

Completed at the Toulouse Cancer Research Center (CRCT) in the NetB(IO)² team led by Vera Pancaldi, funded by Janssen. Focused on pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC), studying tumor microenvironment dynamics.

Multi-Agent Model

Designed a multi-agent model with PhysiCell representing tumor tissue dynamics: epithelial/mesenchymal cells, macrophages, fibroblasts, T lymphocytes. Biological processes modeled include hypoxia, immunosuppression, and epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT). Revealed the central role of M2 macrophages in immune suppression and pancreatic stroma structuring.

Spatial Analysis Pipeline

Developed a spatial tissue analysis pipeline for mIF and IMC images using Tysserand (cell network construction) and MOSNA (multi-omic spatial network analysis). Encapsulated in an interactive graphical interface for laboratory researchers, enabling cell niche detection, assortativity calculation, and structural comparison between patients.

Synthetic Tissue Generation

Initiated work on generating synthetic tissue networks from real data statistics using Markov Random Field (MRF) and Potts models, paving the way for simulated artificial tissues to explore new hypotheses without scarce experimental data.

MORFEE Analyses

Variant annotation pipeline for 5'UTR non-coding mutations in hereditary cardiovascular disease genes.

GitHub
NextflowANNOVARMORFEEPythonUK BiobankGenomics
Context

Completed at the Institut du Thorax (Inserm, Nantes) under Antoine Rimbert's supervision. Focused on non-coding genetic variants in 5'UTR regions of genes involved in familial dyslipidemia: LDLR, APOB, PCSK9, APOE.

Analysis Pipeline

Built a comprehensive pipeline using ANNOVAR for annotating VCF/BCF variants and MORFEE for detecting 5'UTR mutations. Automated with Nextflow to efficiently process large volumes from local cohorts and the UK Biobank (200,000+ individuals). Detected several rare potentially pathogenic variants and extended analysis to the whole human genome.
